Resume Tips

  • Tailor your resume to each job application. Highlight the skills and experiences most relevant to the specific role.
  • Use strong action verbs to describe your responsibilities and achievements (e.g., Developed, Managed, Implemented, Analyzed).
  • Quantify your achievements whenever possible. Instead of saying "Improved sales," say "Increased sales by 15% in the last quarter."
  • Keep your resume concise and easy to read. Aim for one page if you have less than 10 years of experience.
  • Proofread your resume meticulously for any typos or grammatical errors. Ask someone else to review it as well.

Resume Ideas

  • Consider using a clean and professional resume template. There are many free and paid options available online.
  • If you have a portfolio or online presence (e.g., LinkedIn, GitHub), include links to them on your resume.
  • For creative roles, consider a visually appealing resume design that showcases your style (while still being professional).
  • Think about including a brief professional summary or objective statement at the top of your resume to highlight your key qualifications.
  • If you have gaps in your employment history, be prepared to address them honestly and briefly.
